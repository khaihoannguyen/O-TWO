Our Goal

Northeastern aims to leverage the talent from our students, faculty, staff, and global partnerships to implement just climate and sustainability solutions across our campus and communities. We want to make these learnings and information available to you, our community.

Nature’s Guardians of Clean Air

Trees remove air pollution through a remarkable process called "phytoremediation." Leaves, bark, and roots of trees act as filters, absorbing harmful pollutants such as carbon dioxide, sulfur dioxide, and ozone. Trees convert these pollutants into life-sustaining oxygen and beneficial carbohydrates through photosynthesis.

Campus Natural Rooftops

Additionally, tree canopies provide shade, reducing the heat island effect on campus, which can lower energy consumption and decrease the formation of ground-level ozone. In this way, trees play a vital role in improving air quality and creating healthier environments for all of us.
